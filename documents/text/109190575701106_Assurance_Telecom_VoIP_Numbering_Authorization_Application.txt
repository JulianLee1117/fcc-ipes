APPLICATION OF ASSURANCE TELECOM, LLC
FOR AUTHORIZATION TO OBTAIN NUMBERING RESOURCES
Assurance Telecom, LLC (“Assurance Telecom”) pursuant to Section 52.15(g)(3)(i) of
the Commission’s Rules, respectfully requests authorization to obtain numbering resources as an
interconnected VoIP provider. Assurance Telecom seeks authorization by the Commission
through an application containing the information detailed in Section 52.15 (g)(3)(i)(A)-(G) of
the Commission’s Rules. In support of this application, Assurance Telecom provides the
following information:
I. INFORMATION REQUIRED BY SECTION 52.15(g)(3)(i)

B. § 52.15(g)(3)(i)(B)
Assurance Telecom hereby acknowledges that authorization to obtain numbering
resources under Section 52.15(g) of the Commission's Rules is subject to compliance with
applicable Commission numbering rules as well as to the numbering authority delegated to the
states. Assurance Telecom hereby also acknowledges that this authorization is subject to
compliance with industry guidelines and practices regarding numbering, as applicable to
telecommunications carriers.
C. § 52.15(g)(3)(i)(C)

Assurance Telecom acknowledges that it must file requests for numbers with the relevant
state commission(s) at least 30 days before requesting numbers from the Numbering
Administrators.
D. § 52.15(g)(3)(i)(D)
Assurance Telecom hereby sets forth its capability to provide service within 60 days of
the numbering resources activation date. Assurance Telecom currently holds an interconnect
agreement with its carrier partner to provide connectivity to the PSTN. The agreement
demonstrating this capability is attached as Confidential Exhibit A.
E. § 52.15(g)(3)(i)(E)
Assurance Telecom hereby certifies that it will comply with its Universal Service Fund
contribution obligations under 47 CFR part 54, subpart H, its Telecommunications Relay Service
contribution obligations under 47 CFR § 64.604(c)(5)(iii), its North American Numbering Plan
and Local Number Portability Administration contribution obligations under 47 CFR §§ 52.17
and 52.32, its obligations to pay regulatory fees under 47 CFR § 1.1154, and its 911 obligations
under 47 CFR part 9.
F. § 52.15(g)(3)(i)(F)
Assurance Telecom certifies that it has the financial, managerial, and technical expertise
to provide reliable service. It is financially stable, led by a strong, experienced management team
with substantial managerial experience in the telecommunications industry, and has sufficient
technical expertise and infrastructure in place to provide reliable numbering services.
Assurance Telecom’s key management and technical personnel are Jim Dalton, Chief
Executive Officer and Managing Partner, Alcina de Figueiredo, Executive Vice President and
Managing Partner, and Alec Fenichel, Chief Technical Officer. Neither of the identified
personnel are being or have been investigated by the Commission or any law enforcement or
regulatory agency for failure to comply with any law, rule, or order.
G. § 52.15(g)(3)(i)(G)
Assurance Telecom hereby certifies that no party to this application is subject to a denial
of Federal benefits pursuant to Section 5301 of the Anti-Drug Abuse Act of 1988,21 U.S.C. §
862.
II. ACKNOWLEDGEMENT OF CONDITIONS IN SECTION 52.15(g)(3)(iv)
As required by Section 52.15(g)(3)(iv), Assurance Telecom will maintain the accuracy of
all contact information and certifications in this application and will file a correction with the
Commission and each applicable state within 30 days of any changes. Assurance Telecom will
also furnish accurate regulatory and numbering contact information to each state commission
when requesting numbers in that state.

III. CONCLUSION
Pursuant to Section 52.15(g)(3)(i) of the Commission’s Rules, Assurance Telecom
respectfully requests the Commission grant this application for authorization to obtain
numbering resources.
